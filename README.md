# Autism Spectrum Screening Tool

> **This is not a diagnostic tool.** It is a self-reflection instrument designed to help you explore whether certain traits resonate with your experience. Only a qualified clinician can diagnose autism spectrum disorder.

---

## Design Principles

- Based on **DSM-5 criteria** and validated instruments (AQ, RAADS-R, CAT-Q)
- Questions use everyday language — domain labels are hidden from the user
- **4-point Likert scale** (no neutral midpoint) to encourage genuine reflection
- DSM-5 requires deficits in *both* social communication *and* restricted/repetitive behaviors — scoring reflects this
- Masking/camouflaging is tracked as a **modifier**, not a primary axis (especially relevant for women and late-diagnosed adults)

---

## The 30 Questions

All questions use the same four response options:

| Label | Score |
|---|---|
| This is definitely not like me | 0 or 3 (direction varies) |
| This is mostly not like me | 1 or 2 |
| This is mostly like me | 2 or 1 |
| This is definitely like me | 3 or 0 |

Scoring direction is not shown to the user.

## Scoring

| Domain | Max score | Low | Moderate | High |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| A — Social Communication | 36 | < 12 | 12–22 | 23+ |
| B — Restricted & Repetitive | 24 | < 8 | 8–14 | 15+ |
| C — Sensory Processing | 18 | < 6 | 6–10 | 11+ |
| D — Masking *(modifier)* | 12 | < 5 | — | 5+ |

**Primary rule:** Elevation in **both A and B** is required to reflect an autism-consistent profile, mirroring DSM-5. Domain C acts as an amplifier. Domain D is a separate contextual flag.

---

## Result Groups

| Group | Trigger condition | Label |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | A low + B low | Few traits detected |
| 2 | A moderate/high + B low | Social differences without repetitive traits |
| 3 | A low + B moderate/high | Repetitive/sensory traits without social differences |
| 4 | A moderate + B moderate | Moderate autism-consistent traits |
| 5 | A high + B moderate, **or** A moderate + B high | Significant autism-consistent traits |
| 6 | A high + B high | Strong autism-consistent trait profile |

### Masking Modifier

If **D ≥ 5**, the following note is appended to Groups 1–3:

> *Your responses suggest active camouflaging, which can suppress scores even when autism is present. This is especially common in women and people diagnosed later in life. A score in a lower group does not rule out autism if masking is significant.*

---

## Result Messages

**Group 1** — Your responses don't suggest a strong autism trait profile at this time. If something still feels "off", it's always valid to speak with a professional — other conditions like ADHD, anxiety, or sensory processing differences can look similar.

**Group 2** — You show meaningful differences in social communication, but without the repetitive patterns or sensory traits that typically appear alongside autism. These experiences are real and worth exploring — they may relate to social anxiety, ADHD, or other conditions. A psychologist can help clarify this.

**Group 3** — You show sensory sensitivities and/or repetitive patterns without significant social communication differences. This can appear in ADHD, OCD, sensory processing disorder, and sometimes autism. Worth discussing with a professional.

**Group 4** — Your responses show traits in both key areas that clinicians look for in autism — social communication and repetitive patterns. This doesn't mean you are autistic, but it does suggest a professional assessment could be genuinely useful.

**Group 5** — Your responses are notably consistent with an autism spectrum profile. Both the social and repetitive behavior domains are elevated. A formal evaluation by a psychologist or psychiatrist specialising in autism would give you much clearer answers.

**Group 6** — Your responses show a strong and consistent pattern across every domain associated with autism. This is not a diagnosis — but it is a clear signal that professional evaluation is strongly recommended, and worth pursuing.
